It is of great importance of understanding the risk factors for pneumonia that place children at risk of serious outcomes. In this notebook, we explore the child hospitalization data for pneumonia from South Africa to identify the clinical features associated with serious outcomes and prolonged hospitalization.

This notebook showcases the ability to do exploratory analysis within the eLwazi Data Commons. The analysis is not intended to constitute advice nor is it to be used as a substitute for decision making from a professional.

Data description¶

1. Study design¶

A population-based birth cohort established in 2012 in a peri-urban community outside Cape Town, South Africa, has been described in detail. The population is of low socio-economic status; unemployment and maternal HIV infection are common. Pregnant women (20–28 weeks’ gestation) were recruited and followed through pregnancy and delivery; mother-child pairs were followed through early childhood. For this study, follow-up through the first 2 years of life is included.

3. Data description¶

Birth characteristics:¶

  • infant_id: Subject ID.
  • pneumonia date: Date of pneumonia diagnosis.
  • dob: Subject date of birth.
  • prem: Preterm.
  • age_months: Age in month.
  • sex: Sex (Female; Male).
  • lbw: Low birth weight.
  • hiv_exposure: HIV exposure status.

Exposures:¶

  • Maternal smoke category: Non smoker / passive smoker; Active smoker.
  • people in house: Household crowding.
  • vaccinations: Routine vaccinations (All on time; All given but some late; Some doses missed).

Clinical features:¶

  • uwfa: Underweight for age.
  • stunted: Impaired growth and development .
  • temp: Pyrexia > 38°C axillary temp on presentation.
  • resp: Respiratory rate.
  • pulse: Pulse rate.
  • O2 saturation: Oxygen saturation (Hypoxic is defined as saturation <92%).

Chest radiograph findings:¶

  • Xray: Radiographs are classified as no consolidation, “other” consolidation, or “primary end point consolidation”. "duration_admission": "duration of hospitalization",

Laboratory features:¶

  • CRP: C-reactive protein, mg/L.

Outcomes:¶

  • duration_admission: Duration of hospitalization.
  • prolonged: Prolonged hospitalization.
  • serious outcome?: Died or admitted to ICU; Lower Respiratory Tract Infections (LRTI).
  • outcome: Died; Discharged home.

2. Clinical features grouped by age at diagnosis of pneumonia¶

This visualization and accompanying information display the clinical features of pediatric pneumonia patients grouped by age at diagnosis. The age categories used were under 2 months, 2 to 12 months, and over 12 months.

The visualization shows that fatal pneumonia or ICU admission were strongly associated with younger age, with 2 out of 3 deaths and 9 out of 16 ICU admissions occurring in children under 2 months. Additionally, children under 2 months had longer durations of hospitalization compared to children over 12 months.

The information provided also notes that of the 174 pneumonia hospitalizations, 93% had chest radiographs reported. Overall, 40% of these cases had "primary end point consolidation" (PEPC) and 38% had "other consolidation". Interestingly, PEPC was equally common in all age categories.

This visualization and information can be used by healthcare providers to better understand the clinical features of pneumonia patients in different age categories. For example, clinicians may use this information to identify high-risk patients who require more intensive treatment or monitoring, or to develop targeted treatment plans based on the specific clinical features of each patient. Overall, this visualization and accompanying information provide valuable insights into the clinical characteristics of pediatric pneumonia patients grouped by age at diagnosis.

def add_n_obs(df, group_col, y):
    medians_dict = {grp[0]: grp[1][y].median() for grp in df.groupby(group_col)}
    xticklabels = [x.get_text() for x in plt.gca().get_xticklabels()]
    n_obs = df.groupby(group_col)[y].size().values
    for (x, xticklabel), n_ob in zip(enumerate(xticklabels), n_obs):
        plt.text(
            x,
            medians_dict[xticklabel] * 4,
            "n =  " + str(n_ob),
            horizontalalignment="center",
            fontdict={"size": 10},
            color="black",
        )


add_n_obs(data, group_col="vaccinations", y="duration_admission")

plt.show()
